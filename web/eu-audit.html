BCG-GRADE GAP ANALYSIS

What your India audit doesn't cover
— and what it costs you if you miss it

The India compliance framework (TCPA, FTC AI, NIST AI RMF, ADA, WCAG) covers the US market well. Here is every material gap when operating in Europe or serving European customers.

EU AI ACT — TRANSPARENCY
NOT COVERED IN INDIA AUDIT
Article 50 requires AI systems interacting with humans to disclose their AI nature "in a clear and distinguishable manner" before the interaction — stronger than FTC guidance. US audit checks for disclosure but not the EU's specific timing, clarity, and acknowledgment requirements. Voice AI systems must additionally disclose the identity of the deploying natural or legal person on request.
PENALTY: Up to €15M or 3% global turnover · Art. 50 EU AI Act
GDPR ARTICLE 22 — AUTOMATED DECISIONS
NOT COVERED IN INDIA AUDIT
Any voice AI that makes or materially influences decisions about individuals — insurance claims routing, credit inquiries, healthcare triage — triggers Art. 22 rights. Callers have the right to human review, meaningful explanation, and to contest the outcome. The US audit has no equivalent check. This is one of the highest-enforcement GDPR provisions.
PENALTY: Up to €20M or 4% global turnover · GDPR Art. 83(4)
ePRIVACY DIRECTIVE — CALL RECORDING CONSENT
NOT COVERED IN INDIA AUDIT
US TCPA handles unsolicited calls. The EU ePrivacy Directive goes further: explicit informed consent required before recording, strict opt-in for automated marketing calls, and separate consent for voice biometric processing. Many US-trained IVRs record by default — this is illegal without active consent disclosure under EU law in most member states.
PENALTY: Varies by member state; Germany up to €300K per violation
ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI MANAGEMENT SYSTEM
NOT COVERED IN INDIA AUDIT
ISO 42001 is the first international AI management system standard and the primary conformity mechanism referenced by EU AI Act notified bodies. The India audit maps to NIST AI RMF 100-1 and 600-1 but has no ISO 42001 control mapping. EU enterprise buyers are beginning to require ISO 42001 certification or equivalence as a procurement condition.
COMMERCIAL RISK: Loss of EU enterprise procurement eligibility
NIST AI RMF 2.0 — SUPPLY CHAIN & GenAI
PARTIALLY COVERED — GAP EXISTS
India audit covers NIST AI RMF 100-1 and 600-1. The March 2025 update added model provenance, third-party AI component assessment, and supply chain risk — not yet in the India framework. For voice AI using Deepgram, Twilio, or Azure AI, the organization deploying the IVR is the "deployer" under EU AI Act and bears responsibility for third-party model compliance.
REGULATORY RISK: Deployer liability for third-party AI components under Art. 25
EU AI ACT — RISK CLASSIFICATION ASSESSMENT
PARTIALLY COVERED — GAP EXISTS
The EU AI Act requires a formal risk classification before deployment. Voice AI used in insurance, healthcare, financial services, or access to essential services is likely classified as high-risk under Annex III. High-risk systems require conformity assessment, technical documentation, CE marking, and EU database registration before August 2026. No equivalent classification check exists in the India audit.
PENALTY: Up to €30M or 6% global turnover for unregistered high-risk systems
DISCLOSURE TIMING — COVERED WITH UPGRADE
INDIA AUDIT COVERS · EU REQUIRES STRONGER TEST
The India audit's AI disclosure check (35 pts) maps to FTC guidelines and tests for presence of disclosure. EU AI Act Art. 50 requires disclosure "before the interaction begins" and in a manner that cannot be missed. The European audit upgrades this to test timing precision (within first 10 seconds), clarity score, and acknowledgment capability.
UPGRADE: Timing-gated disclosure scoring replaces binary pass/fail
HUMAN ESCAPE PATH — COVERED WITH UPGRADE
INDIA AUDIT COVERS · EU REQUIRES STRONGER TEST
The India audit's escape path check (40 pts) covers TCPA right-to-agent standards. GDPR Art. 22 and EU AI Act require that for automated decision-making, human intervention is not merely available but actively presented to the caller. The European audit adds a check for proactive human offer — not just passive availability.
UPGRADE: Proactive human offer check added to escape scoring
